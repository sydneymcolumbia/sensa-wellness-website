Inflammation & Chronic Disease

Chronic inflammation is now recognized as a shared biological mechanism underlying the majority of the world's most common and deadly diseases. From arterial plaque formation in heart disease to insulin resistance in type 2 diabetes, and from tumor-promoting microenvironments in cancer to joint destruction in arthritis, inflammation is rarely just a symptom. In most cases it is an active driver. This collection brings together the evidence on how chronic inflammation contributes to specific diseases and what elevated CRP levels can signal about your long-term disease risk.

Conditions

How Inflammation Drives Heart Disease: Beyond Cholesterol

CRP and arterial inflammation are now understood to be as important as LDL cholesterol in predicting and driving cardiovascular events.

 Conditions

The Inflammation-Cancer Connection: What Research Reveals

Chronic inflammation creates a cellular environment that promotes DNA damage, tumor growth, and resistance to the immune system's cancer surveillance.

 Conditions

Inflammation and Diabetes: The Connection You Need to Understand

Inflammatory cytokines impair insulin signaling and damage beta cells, making chronic inflammation both a cause and consequence of type 2 diabetes.

 Conditions

Arthritis and Inflammation: Understanding the Root Cause

Both osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis involve sustained inflammatory signaling in joint tissue, though the triggers and pathways differ significantly.

 Conditions

COPD and Inflammation: How Lung Disease Becomes Systemic

COPD is driven by persistent airway inflammation that eventually spills into systemic circulation, raising CRP and increasing the risk of cardiovascular complications.

 Conditions

Obesity and Inflammation: How Excess Fat Drives Chronic Disease

Adipose tissue is metabolically active and secretes pro-inflammatory adipokines, making excess body fat a direct source of chronic systemic inflammation.

 Conditions

Hypertension and Inflammation: The Vascular Inflammatory Loop

Inflammation damages arterial walls and promotes vasoconstriction, while high blood pressure in turn amplifies inflammatory signaling in a self-reinforcing cycle.

 Conditions

Metabolic Syndrome and Inflammation: The Cluster That Predicts Disease

The co-occurrence of visceral obesity, elevated blood sugar, high triglycerides, and hypertension is inseparable from chronic low-grade inflammation.

 Conditions

Kidney Disease and Inflammation: A Two-Way Relationship

Chronic kidney disease both results from and amplifies systemic inflammation, with CRP serving as an important marker of disease progression and cardiovascular risk.

 Conditions

Liver Inflammation: From Fatty Liver to Cirrhosis

The progression from non-alcoholic fatty liver disease to more serious liver conditions is driven by a cascade of inflammatory events tied to metabolic dysfunction.
