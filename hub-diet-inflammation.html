Inflammation & Diet

Every meal either nudges your inflammatory baseline up or down. The foods you eat shape the gut microbiome, regulate immune signaling, and determine how much oxidative stress your cells face each day. Research has identified both clear dietary villains, including added sugar, refined carbohydrates, and ultra-processed foods, and powerful allies, from omega-3 fatty acids and polyphenols to fiber and specific herbs. This collection brings together the evidence on how diet and inflammation are connected, from broad dietary patterns to individual nutrients.

Fermentable fiber feeds beneficial gut bacteria that produce short-chain fatty acids, compounds with potent anti-inflammatory effects throughout the body.

Curcumin and Inflammation: What the Evidence Tells Us

Curcumin, the active compound in turmeric, inhibits NF-kB signaling and has shown measurable CRP reductions in several controlled trials.

Polyphenols and Inflammation: Nature's Anti-Inflammatory Compounds

Plant polyphenols found in berries, green tea, olive oil, and dark chocolate modulate multiple inflammatory pathways and support gut health.

Hydration and Inflammation: Does Drinking More Water Help?

Adequate hydration supports lymphatic clearance, kidney function, and mucosal barriers, all of which influence the body's inflammatory load.
